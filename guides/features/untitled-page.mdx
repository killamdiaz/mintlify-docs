## Introduction

Exhausts lets you create and manage real-time log streams that Atlas Engine can ingest and analyze. Use this to bring external logs (like Zscaler LSS) or custom JSON/NDJSON logs into your workspace for monitoring, debugging, and investigation.

_The Exhausts dashboard lists all log streams along with their status and recent activity._

![Image](/images/image-11.png)

### **What you can do here**

- Create a new log stream (Custom or Zscaler LSS)
- Track stream connectivity (**Active / Waiting / Disconnected**)
- View stream metadata (creator, last activity)
- Link a stream to a support ticket for better traceability

### Create new log stream

Click **Create New Log Stream** to open the stream setup dialog.

![Image](/images/image-12.png)

_Choose an exhaust type, name the stream, and optionally link it to a ticket._

1. **Exhaust Type**

   Choose one:
   - **Custom Log Stream** â€” Generic **JSON/NDJSON ingestion** (best for internal services and custom pipelines)
   - **Zscaler LSS** â€” Use **LSS URL + secret** configured in Zscaler Admin
2. **Stream Name**

   Enter a descriptive name (examples):
   - prod-api-logs
   - zscaler-firewall-lss
   - agent-runtime-events
3. **Link to Ticket (Optional)**

   Attach a ticket to connect the log stream to an incident or work item.

   ![Image](/images/image-13.png)

   _Linking a ticket helps track ownership and troubleshooting context for the stream._

### **Create Stream**

Click **Create Stream** to register the stream. The stream will appear in the table and its status will update as ingestion starts.
